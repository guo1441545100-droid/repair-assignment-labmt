# AI usage log

This is the honest, section-by-section account of what Claude (Anthropic) helped me with during the repair assignment, and what I did myself. The course policy says AI use is allowed if it is disclosed and does not replace my thinking on the research contribution. I have tried to be specific rather than vague. "Used AI to help with coding" is not a disclosure, it is a shrug.

## Overall split

If I had to put a single number on it: roughly **40% of the prose in README.md was drafted or tightened by Claude and then edited by me**, roughly **60% of the Python in `src/` was drafted by Claude from my pseudocode and then debugged together**, and **100% of the research question, the framing of labMT as a four-way comparison, the filter decisions, and the interpretation of the results is mine**. The research-object framing ("use labMT itself as the comparison") is the move I am most proud of in this repair, and it is the one I will not share credit for. It came out of re-reading the group feedback and realising the rubric does not require an outside corpus.

## By file

I asked Claude specifically "explain the superpopulation framing in three sentences that a teacher grading a repair assignment will read" and then cut the result to the two sentences that felt honest to me.

The commentary under each table is mine, with one exception: the two-way reading of Comparison 3 ("lexical-selection" vs "register-averaging") was Claude's suggestion. ### `src/fetch_data.py`

Mine. It is a check-if-file-exists script, there was nothing to delegate.

### `src/load_labmt.py`

Drafted by Claude from my spec ("load the tsv, skip 3 header rows, convert `--` to NaN, add four `in_*` flags and `n_corpora` and `valence_band`"). I edited the column list and the `band()` function (I specifically wanted 4/6 as edges, not 4.5/5.5, because it matches the Δh=1 filter semantics). The `summarize()` helper is all Claude.

### `src/descriptive.py`

Co-written. I had a working first draft from the group project that I mostly discarded. The overlay histogram and the 2×2 small-multiples rank-vs-happiness plot I wrote myself because I knew exactly what I wanted. The overlap-bar and scatter figures are adapted from Claude suggestions. The `summary()` helper is a refactor Claude did for me because my first version was repeating the same pd.describe call five times.

### `src/bootstrap_inference.py`

This is the most collaborative file. I wrote the math part (`boot_diff`, `boot_mean`, the loop structure) from the reference paper. Claude refactored the return signatures to be consistent across the two helpers and added the prob_diff_positive column, which I had forgotten to include. The three `comparison_*` functions I wrote; Claude fixed two bugs (one numpy-shape bug in the initial error bars, one pandas `.loc` boolean indexing bug that was dropping the wrong rows). The `dump_fill_in` function is Claude's suggestion and I accepted it wholesale. It saved me 20 minutes of transcription.

### `src/robustness.py`

Claude drafted the skeleton from the bootstrap_inference pattern. I chose the four conditions. Condition D (drop high-SD words) was my idea and it turned out to be the one that moved the twitter-lyrics result, which is the finding in §6 I care about most. The forest plot layout with four offsets per row I adapted from a matplotlib gallery example; Claude wrote the `zip(conditions, colors, offsets)` loop.

### `src/qualitative_exhibit.py`

Drafted by Claude from my spec ("top 10 happy and top 10 sad exclusive words per corpus, plus a 20-word anchor exhibit across four categories"). I rewrote the `pick()` helper because Claude's first version was iterating over a dict of dataframes and it was getting confused about the category names.

### `src/run_all.py`

Mine. It is six lines of `subprocess.call`.

## Debugging moments I want to flag

- **The n_corpora == 0 bug:** I caught this while transcribing numbers into §3.3 and noticed Comparison 3 had a row for n=0 that did not make sense. Tracked it back to labMT rows like `b-day` and `x-mas`. I fixed it in `bootstrap_inference.py` (only, not in `load_labmt.py`) because I wanted the cleaned CSV to still reflect what the raw file actually contains.
- **The twitter-lyrics D-condition surprise:** I ran the robustness script expecting a boring "yeah, everything replicates" table, and instead saw the twitter-lyrics CI cross zero under Condition D. I went back to the exclusive-words table, found the profanity words that the filter was removing, and rewrote §6 to say so. Without that check I would have reported a finding that was only partly there.
- **A silly matplotlib pitfall:** my first pass at `bootstrap_comparison_1.png` had the corpora on the y-axis in the wrong order because I forgot that `errorbar` draws bottom-up. Claude caught it on the first render.
